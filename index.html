The 4th Trimester Program Calls for Expanded Postpartum Healthcare Access

The 4th Trimester Program is urging policymakers to strengthen postpartum healthcare coverage and expand access to maternal health services across the United States. Many pregnancy-related complications occur in the weeks and months following childbirth, yet millions of women face barriers to receiving adequate follow-up care. By advocating for improved postpartum support and expanded healthcare coverage, the 4th Trimester Program aims to ensure that new mothers receive the medical attention and resources necessary for a safe and healthy recovery.

New Research Highlights Critical Gaps in Postpartum Care

The 4th Trimester Program released new research highlighting the urgent need for stronger postpartum healthcare systems in the United States. The findings emphasize that many mothers experience limited access to care after childbirth, particularly those relying on Medicaid or living in underserved communities. The organization is calling on healthcare leaders and policymakers to prioritize maternal health policies that expand postpartum care and improve long-term health outcomes for mothers nationwide.
